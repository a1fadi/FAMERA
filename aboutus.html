What We Are Solving!

As humans, we all deserve fundamental human rights: safe housing, access to food, water, health, education, and liberty. Ensuring these rights universally has been a long-standing challenge. Safe housing is a vital right, encompassing more than just shelter. It involves a solid structure for adverse weather, access to basic resources, and security against theft and assault. Achieving these aspects of safe housing has been complex, leading to various social issues.

Administering secure housing isn't always feasible due to socioeconomic factors, but we can enhance the security of existing locations, improving mental and physical well-being. This project aims to enhance one of the core human rights, shelter, by focusing on security aspects. Threats to house security include theft, social engineering scams, domestic violence, and online privacy. Common security measures like CCTV cameras and locks mainly deter criminals but often fail to notify homeowners promptly of an intrusion, resulting in missed security breaches.

How are we different?

This project's goal is to reduce Type 2 errors, where crimes go undetected. We aim to improve security, particularly in front yards, by developing a camera system that notifies homeowners in real-time when someone enters their property. It will use facial recognition technology to identify permitted entrants and send instant notifications via Whatsapp. This proactive approach provides peace of mind and the ability to take further action as needed.

While existing technologies like Eufy, Ring, and Wyze offer similar features, the "Fam-era" project simplifies the concept with slight differences, providing an effective solution to enhance home security. This project's mission is to make home security more accessible and efficient, ensuring homeowners are alerted in real-time, thus improving their overall safety and peace of mind.
